What is a Specialty Occupation?

According to USCIS, a specialty occupation requires theoretical and practical application of a body of highly specialized knowledge and the attainment of a bachelor's or higher degree in the specific specialty (or its equivalent). Examples include engineers, IT professionals, scientists, accountants, and architects.

For more details, visit the USCIS website.

Eligibility Summary

H-1B May Not Be Available

Failure to maintain lawful status can render you ineligible for a change of status within the U.S. You may need to depart the U.S. and seek a visa at a consulate, but this issue should be discussed with an experienced immigration attorney.

H-1B Requires Employer Sponsorship

The H-1B visa is employer-sponsored. You must have a job offer from a U.S. employer who is willing to file the petition on your behalf. You cannot self-petition for an H-1B.

Position May Not Qualify

The H-1B visa is strictly for "specialty occupations" that require a bachelor's degree or higher in a specific field. If the position does not meet this standard, it is not eligible for H-1B classification.
